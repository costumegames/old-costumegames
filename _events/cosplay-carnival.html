The UK's largest outdoor Cosplay festival is about to get even bigger with the Cosplay Carnival!

To coincide with the opening weekend of the Rugby World Cup, Brighton Japan will be teaming up with the Brighton & Hove City Council to create the world’s first Cosplay Carnival.

With the support of the award-winning Paraiso School of Samba, the Cosplay Carnival will be launched from “Japan City” in the Old Steine Gardens and proceed down Madeira Drive as part of the festivities planned there.

Perhaps for the first time ever, the wider populace and those who have never heard of Cosplay will be encouraged to take part. People from different social and ethnic backgrounds, the old and the young, will join together for a unique celebration of modern culture.

In the days leading up to the Cosplay Carnival, Brighton Japan will take up residency at the Old Steine Gardens to invite people to prepare their costumes for the Carnival.

We are honoured to have some of the biggest names working today in costumes to help us with this project, including Graham Churchyard, Costume Special Effects Supervisor on the Batman and Harry Potter movies.

Taking place in the city of Gay Pride, the Cosplay Carnival will give renewed expression to the irrepressible spirit of Brighton to celebrate difference.

We look forward to having you on board!
